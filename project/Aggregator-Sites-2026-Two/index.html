Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about starting peptide therapy online.

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules in the body. They naturally occur in humans and are responsible for telling cells how to function. In peptide therapy, specific synthesized peptides are used to mimic these natural signals, potentially aiding in muscle recovery, weight management, anti-aging, and improving cellular function.
Yes. Legitimate, pharmaceutical-grade peptide therapy requires a prescription from a licensed healthcare provider. The platforms listed above connect you with board-certified medical professionals who will evaluate your health profile via a telehealth consultation to determine if peptide therapy is safe and appropriate for you.
When prescribed by a licensed doctor and prepared by a state-licensed, FDA-registered compounding pharmacy, compounded peptides are generally considered safe. While the compounded medications themselves are not individually FDA-approved, the facilities that produce them are highly regulated to ensure sterility, potency, and quality.
The method of administration depends on the specific peptide and your treatment plan. Many common peptides (like GLP-1s, BPC-157, or CJC-1295) are administered via a simple, relatively painless subcutaneous injection (just under the skin) using a tiny needle. Some providers also offer oral troches, nasal sprays, or topical creams for certain types of peptides.
