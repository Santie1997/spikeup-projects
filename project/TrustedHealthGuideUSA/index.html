Common Questions

How much weight will I lose?

Clinical trials have shown that patients on GLP-1 medications can lose an average of 15% of their body weight over 68 weeks when combined with diet and exercise. Results vary by individual.

Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide — what's the difference?

Semaglutide mimics one hormone (GLP-1) to reduce appetite. Tirzepatide mimics two hormones (GLP-1 and GIP), which may lead to greater weight loss efficacy for some patients.

Is this a monthly subscription?

Yes. Long-term consistency is key for metabolic health. Monthly subscriptions ensure you never run out of medication. You can pause or cancel at any time — though stopping often leads to weight regain.

What side effects may occur?

Commonly reported side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and mild fatigue — most noticeable in the initial weeks. Many users report they diminish as the body adjusts to the medication.
